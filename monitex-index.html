1 Technical SEO

The site has problems Google can see before a user ever clicks

Technical issues are the invisible tax on everything else. You can write great content and still not rank if the foundation is broken. Monitex has multiple foundation-level issues.

WordPress-based site with no visible performance optimization

ZoomInfo confirms the site runs on WordPress + PHP hosted behind Cloudflare. Without deliberate optimization, WordPress sites in this segment typically score in the 35–55 range on PageSpeed Insights for mobile. Security company buyers — property managers, construction GCs, warehouse operators — are often searching on mobile mid-job. A slow mobile site sends them to a competitor within 3 seconds.

No evidence of image compression, lazy loading, or caching plugins from any publicly available signal. The site appears to be a standard WordPress install without performance hardening.

Fix

Run a PageSpeed Insights audit on every key page (homepage, each service page). Compress all images to WebP, implement caching (WP Rocket or similar), defer non-critical JavaScript, and use Cloudflare's performance features. Target a mobile LCP under 2.5 seconds.

No structured data (Schema markup)

The site has zero structured data markup. No LocalBusiness, no Service, no FAQPage, no Review schema. This means Google cannot populate rich results for Monitex — no star ratings in search results, no FAQ dropdowns, no service highlights. Competitors who implement this get more real estate on the SERP for free.

Monitex has genuine 5-star reviews from real clients that are living on the website without any schema tagging. Those reviews are invisible to Google's rich snippet system.

Fix

Implement LocalBusiness schema on the homepage and contact page (name, address, phone, geo coordinates, service area). Add Review/AggregateRating schema pulling from existing testimonials. Add FAQPage schema to service pages and the FAQ section. This can be done with a WordPress plugin or direct JSON-LD injection.

robots.txt is blocking crawlers (redirects loop on fetch)

When attempting to crawl monitexsecurity.com, the robots.txt file triggers a redirect loop — indicating a misconfiguration that may be preventing parts of the site from being crawled at all. This is a critical signal. If Google's crawler is being bounced, pages may not be indexed, or may be indexed with lower confidence.

Fix

Audit the robots.txt file immediately. Confirm it is not accidentally disallowing important directories. Verify all canonical tags are correct and that no pages are accidentally set to noindex via Yoast or another plugin. Check Google Search Console for crawl errors and coverage issues.

No XML sitemap confirmed, no GSC verification visible

There is no evidence of a submitted XML sitemap from available signals. Without a sitemap, Google discovers pages purely through crawling — meaning new content and new service pages may not get indexed for weeks. Google Search Console appears either unverified or unmonitored given the crawl configuration issue above.

Fix

Generate and submit an XML sitemap through Yoast or Rank Math. Verify the site in Google Search Console. Submit the sitemap. Monitor coverage reports weekly, especially after new content is published.

No HTTPS security certificate issue visible, but no HSTS confirmed

The site uses Cloudflare, which typically provides SSL. However, HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) headers are not confirmed. Without HSTS, browsers are not forced into HTTPS on repeat visits, which is a minor but real signal to Google on security posture.

Fix

Enable HSTS through Cloudflare's security settings. Ensure SSL mode is set to "Full (Strict)" not just "Flexible."

2 On-Page SEO

The content is not built to rank. It is built to describe.

There is a big difference between content that explains what a company does and content that shows up when someone searches for it. Right now, Monitex has the former. None of the pages are structured around what a potential buyer actually types into Google.

Homepage title tag and meta description are not optimized

The homepage appears to target only the brand name and generic terms. There is no evidence the title tag includes high-volume commercial keywords like "remote video monitoring New York," "live surveillance service," or "virtual security guard service." The meta description does not appear to include a clear call to action or target keyword.

The title tag is what Google shows in search results. If it does not match what buyers search for, the site does not show up — even if the content below is good.

Fix

Rewrite the homepage title tag to lead with the primary commercial keyword and location. Example structure: "Live Remote Video Monitoring | Virtual Security Guards | New York | Monitex Security." Keep under 60 characters. Write a meta description under 155 characters that names the benefit and includes a CTA ("Get a free trial").

Service pages exist but are thin and not keyword-targeted

Monitex has individual pages for residential buildings, construction sites, car dealerships, etc. But each page contains roughly 300–400 words of generic copy. These pages are not ranking for the commercial queries buyers use. A property manager searching "live video monitoring for apartment building NYC" will not find Monitex because the page does not match that query pattern.

Competitors like Pro-Vigil and Live Lion have deeper, more targeted service pages with clear H1 tags, subheadings covering buyer questions, and explicit geographic references.

Fix

Expand each service page to 800–1200 words. Include: a keyword-targeted H1, 3–4 subheadings addressing specific buyer pain points (cost of theft, liability exposure, guard replacement cost), geographic references (New York, NJ, PA markets), a comparison to traditional guards, and a CTA with a phone number or form. Each page should target one primary keyword and 2–3 supporting terms.

No blog or content marketing — zero informational traffic

Monitex has no blog. There is a Media Library with social-style video clips, but no written content targeting informational search queries. This means the site captures zero traffic from buyers who are researching ("how much does remote video monitoring cost," "virtual guard vs security guard," "best surveillance for construction site") before they are ready to buy.

Content marketing is how you build domain authority and capture buyers earlier in the funnel. Every month without it is market share handed to competitors who do have blogs.

Fix

Launch a blog section with at minimum 2 posts per month. Priority topics: cost comparisons (virtual guard vs on-site guard), vertical-specific guides (construction site security, apartment building theft prevention), local crime statistics tied to monitoring need, and FAQ-style posts targeting high-volume queries. These pages build authority and drive qualified traffic that service pages cannot capture.

Internal linking structure is flat — no link equity flowing to key pages

The site's navigation points to service pages, but there is no systematic internal linking between content. This means Google does not receive clear signals about which pages are most important. Service pages are not cross-linking to each other or to the homepage in ways that build ranking weight.

Fix

Build a deliberate internal link structure. Every service page should link to at least 2–3 related service pages. The homepage should link directly to the highest-priority commercial pages. When the blog launches, every post should link to the most relevant service page with keyword-rich anchor text.

Conversion rate optimization: site has a phone number but no clear primary CTA path

The site mentions a phone number and a free trial offer, which is good. However, there is no sticky CTA, no prominently placed lead form above the fold, and no clear funnel guiding high-intent visitors from landing to contact. The "free week's trial" offer is buried in copy rather than being the dominant call to action throughout the site.

Fix

Add a sticky header bar with a click-to-call button and a "Get Free Trial" CTA that persists on scroll. Add a lead capture form above the fold on the homepage. Place CTA buttons at the bottom of every service page section. Test a chatbot or quick-quote widget for property managers who want a fast estimate.

3 Local SEO

A New York company that is invisible in New York search results

Monitex is headquartered in Brooklyn and its core market is NY, NJ, and PA. But local SEO is essentially untouched. Any property manager in New York City searching Google Maps for surveillance services will find competitors first.

Google Business Profile not verified or optimized

Monitex has a BBB listing showing a Brooklyn, NY address. But there is no evidence of an active, fully optimized Google Business Profile. This is the single most important local SEO asset. Without it, Monitex does not appear in the Google Maps 3-pack when someone searches "remote video monitoring Brooklyn" or "security camera monitoring NYC." Those searches have direct commercial intent — they come from buyers who are ready to sign a contract.

Fix

Claim and verify the Google Business Profile immediately. Complete every field: primary category (Security System Supplier), service areas (all of NY, NJ, PA), business hours (24/7), phone number matching the website, website URL, and a detailed service description with keywords. Upload at least 10 photos of actual operations, team, and client properties (with permission). Solicit reviews from existing clients directly to the GBP.

No citation consistency across directories

Monitex has listings on Angi, ZoomInfo, BBB, Crunchbase, RocketReach, and LinkedIn — but there is inconsistency in how the business name, address, and phone number (NAP) appear across these. ZoomInfo shows one data set, BBB shows another ownership structure, and the Facebook page shows yet a different categorization. Google cross-references all of these. Inconsistent NAP data hurts local pack rankings.

Fix

Conduct a full NAP audit across all directory listings. Standardize the exact business name (Monitex Security), address (Brooklyn, NY), and primary phone number. Submit or correct listings on the 10 most important directories: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Yelp, Angi, BBB, Apple Maps, Facebook, LinkedIn, Nextdoor, and Alignable.

No location-specific landing pages

Monitex serves New York, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania with premium services, and the rest of the US at scale. The website has no location-specific pages. There is no "Remote Video Monitoring New York City" page, no "Security Monitoring New Jersey" page. These pages are how you rank in city-level searches, which are the highest-intent searches in this category.

Fix

Create location pages for the top 5–8 markets: New York City, Brooklyn, New Jersey, Philadelphia, Long Island, the Bronx, Queens, and Manhattan. Each page should be 600–900 words, include local references (local crime stats, neighborhood names, borough-specific context), a local phone number if possible, and embedded Google Maps. These pages will rank for "[city] + remote monitoring" queries within 3–6 months of indexing.

Reviews are on the website but not driving Google signals

Monitex has strong client testimonials on its own website, plus a 1-review Facebook page and a new Angi listing with no reviews. This is a missed opportunity. Google's local algorithm weighs review quantity and recency heavily. Clients are clearly happy — the testimonials on the site prove that — but they have not been directed to leave Google reviews.

Fix

Build a review generation system: send every new client a direct link to the Google Business Profile review page after their first successful month. Use WhatsApp (which Monitex already uses for client communication) to send a polite review request. Target 20+ Google reviews within 90 days. This single action will boost local pack rankings faster than most other changes.

4 Authority & Backlinks

Almost no one on the internet is linking to Monitex

Backlinks are Google's primary trust signal. The fewer quality sites that link to you, the harder it is to rank for competitive keywords. Monitex's backlink profile is essentially nonexistent outside a handful of directory and social listings.

Near-zero referring domain count from quality sources

The visible backlink footprint for monitexsecurity.com consists primarily of directory listings (ZoomInfo, Crunchbase, BBB, Angi), the company's own social profiles, and one industry press piece (Business Management Review, July 2025). There are no links from news coverage, security industry publications, property management trade sites, real estate associations, or relevant local business sites. This puts Monitex's estimated Domain Rating in the 5–15 range — essentially starting from zero in Google's eyes when competing for non-branded queries.

Fix

Build links through three channels simultaneously: (1) Pitch security, property management, and real estate trade publications with data-backed articles — crime prevention statistics, cost of on-site guards vs virtual monitoring, case studies. (2) Get listed in relevant industry directories — ASIS International, BOMA (Building Owners and Managers Association), local chamber of commerce. (3) Pursue unlinked brand mentions — any publication that has mentioned Monitex without linking should be contacted for a link addition.

No digital PR or media strategy

Monitex has genuinely compelling video content — real crime prevention footage from their monitoring team. This content is being used on LinkedIn and Facebook but has not been pitched to media. Local news outlets regularly run security-related stories. A single placement in a New York real estate, property management, or local news publication would deliver both a quality backlink and brand exposure to the target audience.

Fix

Develop a media outreach plan. Identify 15–20 journalists covering NYC real estate, property management, and local crime prevention. Pitch specific story angles: "Brooklyn security firm catches over 300 would-be burglars in one year," "The real cost of not having surveillance on your construction site," "Property managers share why they switched from guards to virtual monitoring." Each placement earns a backlink and drives referral traffic from a highly relevant audience.

7 Content & Trust Gaps

Assets Monitex has but is not using — and what competitors are doing instead

This section covers what is missing from the site that a high-converting B2B security website in this niche needs to have.

🏆

No credentialing or certification displayed

Monitex was recognized as "Top Remote Video Surveillance Solution 2025" by Business Management Review. This is nowhere prominent on the website. Pro-Vigil leads with its Five Diamond CSAA certification on the homepage. Trust signals need to be front and center, not buried.

📊

Stats on homepage lack credibility framing

The homepage shows counters for "crimes deterred" and "perpetrators arrested" — but the numbers appear to start from zero as animations. There is no sourcing, no time frame stated. Buyers who are skeptical need anchored, specific numbers: "312 crimes prevented in 2024 across 47 properties."

📋

No pricing page or ballpark cost guidance

Every competitor mentions the "60–70% cheaper than guards" figure — and Monitex mentions it too. But no page on the site gives buyers any framework for what to expect to pay. A pricing page (even one saying "contact for custom quote") signals transparency and ranks for cost-related searches.

🎬

Video content exists but is not SEO-optimized

The crime prevention clips on LinkedIn and the media library are engaging. But videos on the website are not given title tags, descriptions, or transcripts. YouTube SEO for these clips ("thief caught on camera New York construction site") could drive significant traffic and brand awareness independently.

🗺

No case studies or client success stories formatted for SEO

The testimonials are good but short. Deep Sentinel and Pro-Vigil publish full case studies: "How we helped [property type] reduce theft by X%." These rank for industry-specific searches and give the sales team a leave-behind. Monitex has the stories — the Business Management Review article proves it — they just need to be formatted correctly.

🔒

Insurance and liability claims not substantiated

The site mentions Monitex is "fully insured" but gives no details. Live Lion leads with "$1 million guarantee." Buyers in the commercial space care deeply about this. A clear insurance page or callout explaining coverage limits, bonding, and liability policy would remove a major objection and improve conversion.

8 Bottom Line

What this adds up to

Monitex is a proven, growing business with real results and happy clients. The SEO situation is not a sign the business is failing — it is a sign the business has been growing through referrals and outbound sales, and has not yet needed SEO. That changes as competition intensifies and buyers increasingly research online before calling anyone.

The opportunity cost of inaction is compounding

Every month that passes without SEO investment is a month that ECAM, Pro-Vigil, and Live Lion are building backlinks, publishing content, and collecting Google reviews. Authority is not built overnight — which means the sooner work starts, the sooner results compound.

The good news: because Monitex is starting from a low baseline, even moderate investment in the areas identified above will produce visible ranking movement within 60–90 days. The easiest wins (GBP optimization, review generation, title tag fixes, schema markup) can be implemented in under two weeks and will produce measurable local search improvement within 30 days.

Priority action list — first 30 days

  1. Claim and fully optimize Google Business Profile — this is the single highest-ROI action available.
  2. Fix the robots.txt crawl issue — nothing else matters if Google cannot crawl the site reliably.
  3. Rewrite all page title tags and meta descriptions with keyword-targeted, location-aware copy.
  4. Implement schema markup — LocalBusiness, Review, and FAQPage schemas site-wide.
  5. Launch a Google review campaign — contact all existing clients via WhatsApp with a direct review link. Target 20 reviews in 60 days.
  6. Run a PageSpeed audit on all key pages and fix critical performance issues. Target sub-2.5s LCP on mobile.
  7. Publish 4–6 keyword-targeted blog posts hitting the high-priority informational queries above.
